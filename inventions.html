Inventions

1. Apple I (1976): Produced in limited quantities, Apple's first product was a computer designed for engineers and enthusiasts.

2. Apple II (1977): Designed as a mass-market device rather than one for engineers or hobbyists, the Apple II was one of the first commercially successful personal computers.

3. Lisa (1983): Featuring windows, icons, and a mouse-controlled cursor, this was the first commercial computer to have a graphical user interface. The Lisa was the name of it.

4. Macintosh (1984) - The Macintosh had a graphical user interface, just as the Lisa. In addition, it was quicker, less expensive, and supported by a significant marketing campaign.

5. NeXT computer (1989) - Jobs founded a business that produced a potent workstation computer after his departure from Apple.

6. Although it wasn't the first hard drive-equipped digital music player, the iPod (2001) was the first to be commercially successful.

7. iTunes store (2003) - Prior to the iTunes store, purchasing digital music was difficult, leading to a greater preference for piracy. In 2008, the store grew to be the biggest music retailer in the United States.

8. iPhone (2007) - The iPhone simplified the process of unlocking the capabilities of a smartphone, doing for the phone experience what the Macintosh accomplished for personal computing.

9. iPad (2010) - Before the iPad, dozens of firms, including Apple, had developed tablet computers, but none of them were commercially successful. When the iPad eventually figured out the code, a whole new class of computers was born.
