Biography

Born on February 24, 1955, Steve Jobs was recognized as one of Apple Inc.'s co-founders, but unfortunately, he passed away in 2011. During his youth, Steve was raised by adoptive parents in Silicon Valley, California. He started gaining an interest in engineering, so he went to Reed College. However, he soon dropped out to become a video game designer with Atari Corporation.

Later, in 1974, Jobs reconnected with an old high school friend named Stephen Wozniak. The two had ideas to create a new computer logic board, so they worked out of Steve’s family garage. Finally, in 1976, their first prototype, Apple 1, was created. It was funded by selling Steve’s Volkswagon and Stephen’s programable calculator. This was a great success and was one of the leading factors in people having personal computers.

During this time, Steve also started experimenting with using computers to make graphics easier. This led him to start working with Pixar, an animation studio that made movies. Jobs acquired a large portion of this company and began implementing computers into animation. Toy Story is Pixar’s first fully computer-animated movie. Then, in 2006, Jobs sold the company to Disney.

In 1996, Apple encountered substantial financial losses, and then Jobs became Apple’s CEO, which helped revive the company. Over the years, Apple has changed the world with revolutionary products like the iPad, iPhone, AirPods etc. Unfortunately, on October 5, 2011, Steve Jobs died due to some medical implications. However, at his death, he was one of the wealthiest men alive, with a net worth of 8.3 billion dollars. Overall, Stever Jobs changed the world of computers, and his work will be remembered forever.
